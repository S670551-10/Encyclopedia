ISRO: India’s Space Power

ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) is India’s national space agency, founded on 15 August 1969 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai. Its main goal is to use space technology for India’s development.

ISRO launched its first satellite Aryabhata in 1975. In 1980, India became the 7th nation to launch a satellite using its own rocket (SLV-3).

Key missions include :

  • Chandrayaan-1 (2008) – Found water on the Moon

  • Mangalyaan (2013) – Reached Mars on first attempt

  • Chandrayaan-3 (2023) – First to land on Moon’s south pole

  • PSLV-C37 (2017) – Launched 104 satellites at once (world record)

ISRO is known for low-cost, high-success missions, and has helped over 30 countries launch satellites. Its upcoming project is Gaganyaan, India’s first human spaceflight mission.
