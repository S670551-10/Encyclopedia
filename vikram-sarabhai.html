Vikram Sarabhai

Vikram Sarabhai (1919–1971) was an Indian physicist and the founder of India’s space program. Born in Ahmedabad, he studied physics in Cambridge and later returned to India to develop space science.

He started the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), which became ISRO. Sarabhai helped launch India’s first rockets and used space technology to support education and development, especially in rural areas.

He also helped establish important institutes like the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL) and Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad (IIMA).

Sarabhai is remembered as a visionary scientist whose work shaped modern India’s space efforts. The Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre is named in his honor.
